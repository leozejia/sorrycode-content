# Create a Personal Intro

<h2 id="goal">Goal</h2>

Create a one-page intro for someone meeting you for the first time.

This is not a full resume. It is a lighter artifact that explains who you are, what you do, and how you can help.

<h2 id="prepare">What You Need</h2>

- [Kami](/docs/skills/kami) installed
- your basic introduction
- one phrase you want people to remember about you

<h2 id="steps">Steps</h2>

1. Fill in the raw information below
2. Ask `Kami` for the page structure first
3. Choose a tone
4. Confirm the structure before generating the final artifact
5. Ask it to remove vague claims

<h2 id="inputs">Fill This In</h2>

```text
Who I am:
What I do now:
What I am good at:
Representative work, experience, or projects:
What people can contact me for:
One sentence I want people to remember:
Tone: professional / warm / creator-style / formal
```

<h2 id="prompt">Copy This</h2>

```text
Turn the information below into a one-pager personal intro for someone meeting me for the first time. Give me the page structure first, then wait for my confirmation before generating the final artifact.
```

<h2 id="done">Completion Check</h2>

The personal intro should make clear:

- who you are
- how you can help
- what proof or work supports the intro
- how someone should contact or work with you

<h2 id="stuck">If You Get Stuck</h2>

If it reads like a resume, say: `Do not write this like a resume. Write it like an introduction for a potential collaborator.`

If it feels vague, say: `Ask me 5 questions first to uncover concrete experience and representative work.`
