# Organize a Long Article

<h2 id="goal">Goal</h2>

Turn messy notes, interviews, drafts, or research material into a structured long document.

This is not just summarization. The goal is to reorganize material into an article a reader can follow.

<h2 id="prepare">What You Need</h2>

- [Kami](/docs/skills/kami) installed
- source material
- a target reader

<h2 id="steps">Steps</h2>

1. Tell `Kami` the goal and target reader
2. Paste the source material
3. Ask for structure and editorial decisions first
4. Confirm the structure before writing the article
5. Ask it to check the title, sections, and key points

<h2 id="inputs">Fill This In</h2>

```text
Article topic:
Target reader:
Source material:
Key points to preserve:
What to remove or downplay:
Final use: publishing / internal sharing / study notes / report
Tone: professional / casual / deep analysis / personal experience
```

<h2 id="prompt">Copy This</h2>

```text
Turn the material below into a formal long article for the target reader. Give me the article structure and editorial decisions first, then wait for my confirmation before writing the body.
```

<h2 id="done">Completion Check</h2>

The article should have:

- a clear title and sections
- real editorial choices instead of a pile of material
- every section serving the same topic
- a draft you can continue editing, not just a summary

<h2 id="stuck">If You Get Stuck</h2>

If it misses important material, say: `List the 10 most important points in the source material first. Do not write the article yet.`

If it is too long, say: `Keep the structure, but compress each section into 2 to 3 paragraphs.`
